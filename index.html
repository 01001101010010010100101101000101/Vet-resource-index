❓ Frequently Asked Questions

How do I enroll in VA health care?
Apply online at VA.gov, call 1-877-222-8387, or visit your nearest VA medical center. Most veterans who served on active duty and were separated under conditions other than dishonorable are eligible. Priority groups are assigned based on disability rating, income, and other factors.
How do I file a VA disability claim?
File online through VA.gov using VA Form 21-526EZ, by mail, or in person at a VA regional office. A free accredited VSO (DAV, VFW, American Legion) can help you build the strongest possible claim at no cost. Gather service records, medical evidence, and a nexus letter from a doctor linking your condition to service.
What if my VA claim is denied?
You have three appeal options: (1) Supplemental Claim — submit new evidence; (2) Higher-Level Review — a senior reviewer looks at your file; (3) Board of Veterans' Appeals — request a hearing before a Veterans Law Judge. You generally have one year from the denial letter to appeal. A VSO or accredited attorney can assist at no upfront cost.
Can I use my GI Bill benefits after age 60 or decades after service?
Post-9/11 GI Bill benefits generally do not expire (if you were discharged after January 1, 2013, the old 10-year limit was removed). Montgomery GI Bill benefits expire 10 years after separation. Check your specific chapter and dates at VA.gov.
What benefits are available for National Guard and Reserve members?
Guard and Reserve members may qualify for many VA benefits if they were federally activated under Title 10. This includes VA health care, GI Bill (Chapter 1606 for Reserve; Post-9/11 if activated), disability compensation for service-connected conditions incurred on active orders, and home loan guarantee. Contact your unit's benefits advisor or a VSO for a full eligibility review.
Are family members and survivors eligible for any benefits?
Yes. Survivors and dependents may qualify for: Dependency and Indemnity Compensation (DIC), Survivor Benefit Plan (SBP) annuity, Fry Scholarship and DEA education benefits, CHAMPVA health coverage, burial benefits, and access to military installations. Visit VA Family Member Benefits.
How do I get a copy of my DD-214 discharge papers?
Request your DD-214 online through the National Archives eVetRecs system or through VA.gov. Processing typically takes a few weeks. For urgent needs (e.g., VA claims), note "Expedite — VA claim" in your request.
