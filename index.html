❓ Frequently Asked Questions

How do I enroll in VA health care?
Apply online at VA.gov, call 1-877-222-8387, or visit your nearest VA medical center. Most veterans who served on active duty and were separated under conditions other than dishonorable are eligible. Priority groups are assigned based on disability rating, income, and other factors.
How do I file a VA disability claim?
File online through VA.gov using VA Form 21-526EZ, by mail, or in person at a VA regional office. A free accredited VSO (DAV, VFW, American Legion) can help you build the strongest possible claim at no cost.
What if my VA claim is denied?
You have three appeal options: (1) Supplemental Claim — submit new evidence; (2) Higher-Level Review; (3) Board of Veterans' Appeals. You generally have one year from the denial to appeal. A VSO or accredited attorney can assist at no upfront cost.
Can I use my GI Bill benefits years after service?
Post-9/11 GI Bill benefits do not expire if you were discharged after January 1, 2013. Montgomery GI Bill benefits expire 10 years after separation. Check your specific benefits at VA.gov.
What benefits are available for National Guard and Reserve members?
Guard and Reserve members may qualify for many VA benefits if federally activated under Title 10 — including VA health care, GI Bill (Chapter 1606 or Post-9/11 if activated), disability compensation, and home loan guarantee. Contact a VSO for a full eligibility review.
Are family members and survivors eligible for benefits?
Yes. Survivors and dependents may qualify for DIC, SBP annuity, Fry Scholarship, DEA education benefits, CHAMPVA health coverage, and burial benefits. Visit VA Family Member Benefits.
How do I get a copy of my DD-214?
Request via the National Archives eVetRecs system or VA.gov. For urgent needs (e.g., VA claims), note "Expedite — VA claim" in your request.
